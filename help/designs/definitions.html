Definition of terms used in RSF

Artwork sight line width/height

The visible part of an artwork after it is placed in a frame/board.

Border

The border of a board is equal to the sum of the board's face and the board's overlap

Calculated Overlap

The caltulated overlap is the smallest overlap that can be used without causing technical problems when framing. This is particularly helpful when a limited amounts of board is available.

Competition factor

The competition factor allows business owners to alter the price of specific material items when the price should differ from the normal markup. This is best explained by way of example: A business owner may decide to lower the price of a particular item and not use the general markup. In this instance, the competition factor should be a value less than 1. On the other hand, a material item may be very fragile. The business owner may then decide to price the item above the normal markup in order to compensate for the handling risk. The competition factor should then be a value higher than 1. The default value is 1, meaning that the competition factor has no effect on the price.

Exquisite factor

The price of a particular frame is multiplied by its exquisite factor to take into account any features that should increase its price in excess of the normal markup.

Frame rebate

The distance that the frame will overlap with the item to its inside.

Item code

The code used by RSF as a unique identifier of a particular material item. The item code is the same code used by the suppliers.

Item Face

The visible section of an item in a design.

Markup

The amount with which the cost of a particular item will be multiplied in order to calculate the selling price.

Material Item

Material items are the different materials that make up a design. A design consisting of two mount boards, one frame, one back and a glass contains five material items. Each material item has specific attributes like supplier, length, width, price, etc.

Markup for discount

To be used as a selling aid. The user can increase the markup of a particular item to be able to give the customer a discount.

Offset

The offset of a floating item is the distance between the floating item's outer edge and the outer neighbour's inner edge.

Overlap

The section of an item in a design that is not visible, i.e. it is the section behind the item's outside neighbour.

Own code

If another coding system is required to identify materials, the own code can be used. If own codes are not used, RSF will assign own codes.
